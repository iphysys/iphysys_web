The shift

Physical systems are becoming intelligent. A new generation of robots, sensors, and infrastructure is shifting from procedural control to autonomous behavior. iPhySys is being built for that shift — treating autonomy, distributed decision making, edge intelligence, and trustworthy AI as first-class engineering problems.

Capability areas

  • Multi-Agent Intelligence — Coordination and reasoning across distributed autonomous agents.
  • Edge AI — Deploying intelligence where the mission happens.
  • Distributed Optimization — Planning and optimization under real-world constraints.
  • Human-Machine Teaming — Decision support systems that augment operators.
  • Engineering Intelligence — Transforming intent into trustworthy engineering artifacts.
  • Trustworthy Autonomy — Confidence, risk awareness, and explainability for mission-critical systems.

Technology principles

  • Human-Centered Autonomy — Autonomy that augments human judgment, not replaces it.
  • Intelligence at the Edge — Decisions belong where the physics lives.
  • Distributed by Design — Systems composed of cooperating agents.
  • Explainability Matters — Behavior that can be inspected, audited, and trusted.
  • Mission Awareness — Software that understands intent and consequence.
  • Engineering Rigor — Specification, verification, and operational reality, end to end.

Vision roadmap

  • Today — Research, knowledge development, algorithm exploration, prototype demonstrations.
  • Emerging capabilities — Simulation environments, edge deployments, distributed coordination demonstrations.
  • Future systems — Trusted autonomy, mission systems, large-scale intelligent physical systems.
